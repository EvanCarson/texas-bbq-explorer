Case Study · March 2026

Planning a Tripwith Claude

From a blank conversation to a fully planned, deployed, shareable road trip — in one extended session

How It Started

We had a week-long Texas road trip roughed out — flights booked, some hotels confirmed, a list of BBQ spots, and a bunch of open questions: are the tickets booked? Where do the kids play? Does the route even make sense? What we didn't have was anyone — or anything — to look at the whole picture at once and tell us if it held together.

So we turned to Claude. Not just to generate text, but to act as a trip agent: ingest the raw plan, surface gaps, validate the routing, suggest options, and eventually produce this interactive page the whole family can reference. The conversation below is how it actually went.

What Made This Work
🗂️
Structured data from messy notes
Pasted an unstructured markdown dump — flights, hotels, research, day notes — and Claude extracted it into typed, navigable trip data.
🗺️
Route-aware suggestions
Kalahari was recommended not just for quality, but because Round Rock was already on the routing. Claude cross-checked geography before suggesting.
🎨
Full interactive site, zero frameworks
Built a 5-tab SPA with Leaflet maps, animated day cards, and hotel-to-restaurant directions. Vanilla HTML/CSS/JS — no build step, no dependencies.
🚀
Live URL in minutes
Deployed to Vercel autonomously. Each round of changes produced a new production URL in under two minutes.
🔒
Privacy by default
When asked to remove the family name, Claude found every instance across the document and replaced them — without touching surrounding content.
🌿
Clean git history
All work in an isolated worktree. Incremental commits. Merged to main when done. The repo stays clean throughout.
